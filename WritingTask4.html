How to Carve a Pumpkin:

Carving a pumpkin is a fun seasonal event. It is a good activity for all ages. It can be a bit tricky to master. Some people compete to see who can carve the most intricate or creative design. It is a messy activity so be sure to lay down newspaper on your carving surface and wash your hands afterward. It takes a bit of time to do, so make it an evening activity. Here is some information to get you started.

What you will need:

  • 1 pumpkin
  • Newspaper or anything to be able to put underneath the pumpkin.
  • A marker or something.
  • Pumpkin carving kit.
  • 2 bowls for dumping out the seeds and guts.
  • Towel.
  • Any type of lighter that has an extended nozzle.

    • Instructions: Set up your workspace: Line a sturdy table with flattened grocery bags, newsprint, or butcher paper. Draw your design: Once you have decided how the pumpkin will look like draw it on the pumpkin with a marker. Draw your lid: Draw an outline around the stem. Cut out the pumpkin lid: With a slim pumpkin carving knife or serrated knife, cut along the outline of your pumpkin lid. Remove the pumpkin seeds: The seeds are all attached to the pumpkin and each other by thin strings. Remove them and put them into one of the bowls. Scoop out the insides of the pumpkin: Clean out the inside of the pumpkin until no stringy bits remain. Put the pumpkin guts onto the other bowl. Wipe off the pumpkin: Use the kitchen towel to wipe off the outside of the pumpkin so that it will be easier and safer to carve. Cut out the design: Make straight cuts into your pumpkin along the lines of your design, while you take out the spare parts coming off from the pumpkin Clean up the details: Go back in and scrape out any stringy pieces or jagged lines with a wire tool from your carving kit. Light your pumpkin: Insert a tea light candle in the bottom of your pumpkin. Use a long match or lighter to light the pumpkin and replace the lid.
