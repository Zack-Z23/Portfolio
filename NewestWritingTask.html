A guide on picking a Racing Wheel:

Requirements For a Racing Wheel: There are different requirements that need to be met if you have just started out in Sim Racing. Someone who has just started needs to learn how to drive fast on a wheel. There are many variables while picking a wheel, like:

  • Force Feedback
  • Rotation Degrees
  • Whether you want to drive a manual
  • Whether you want a third pedal(Clutch)
  • What type of profession you wanna do with the wheel(Casually having fun, Racing for money, Getting into Esports)

All these variables are all determined by how you wanna take Sim Racing. Some people have gotten jobs in sports like F1, Nascar, Rally, Motocross all because of them Sim Racing. You can also just use the wheel for fun because you can waste hours on it. So for a price range of 0 - 100 is where most people buy wheels for very young age kids, whilst a price range between 100 - 200 is where the beginner wheels are and they can be very good with Force feedback and different variations of rotation. Before I go any more into detail I might need to explain what Force Feedback is.

Force Feedback: In case you were wondering what Force Feedback then this should be a short explanation. Force Feedback is a feature that some wheels have. Force Feedback is basically stimulating the feel of a real car. Because it is more of something you can experience it is a little difficult to explain it to someone who never has experienced it before, you are able to feel what the car feels when it goes over bumps or curbs or anything that you can in real life while driving your car. This allows for more precision while driving. When I mean precision I mean that, because you can feel the car about to spin out then see the car almost spinning out. This really can help you with being more consistent while driving the car in-game and being able to get on the gas faster than others without Force Feedback.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, get a cheap wheel if you don't want to take your path on sim racing seriously and just want to have fun. Having fun, was at first the main thing sim racing was. It was mainly used for putting yourself into the gaming world just how Virtual Reality with the new headsets that come out and immerse you more into the video game world. Sim racing wheels can be very hefty in the sense of money. The price ranges I listed were the ones where most people would go for trying out wheels, the most I have ever spent was 200 dollars on a wheel and it was worth it. There can be wheels that have Direct Drive. Direct drive is basically a wheel and pedals being so immersive that it makes you think that you are in the situation your car is in. Most professional sim racers, who race for money, use direct drive wheels because being able to feel what the car feels is more information for the driver, meaning you can drive faster at parts of a track that some people can only see the conditions whilst the direct-drive users can feel the conditions and handle it much better.
